## The Principle

Busy teams do not need more interface. They need better attention management.

In operational products, the user is often moving between a task, a physical space, another person, and a policy. The interface has to support that context. It should show what matters, reduce ambiguity, and stay out of the way when the user already knows what to do.

## What Calm Means

Calm does not mean empty. It means the product has a clear hierarchy:

- the current state is easy to read
- the next action is visible
- secondary details are available without crowding the screen
- errors are specific
- confirmations are proportionate to risk

## Why It Matters

When software supports real-world operations, visual clutter becomes operational cost. A confusing table, vague status, or noisy notification can slow down the person responsible for the next step.

The best product work often feels quiet because the decisions have already been made on behalf of the user.
