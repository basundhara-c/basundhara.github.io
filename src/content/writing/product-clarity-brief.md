## Purpose

This document is the public-writing counterpart to private career evidence. The goal is to turn dense proof into clear portfolio material without exposing legal, immigration, employer-confidential, or overly personal details.

## What Belongs On The Site

The site should emphasize:

- product judgment
- operational clarity
- writing quality
- cross-functional work
- systems thinking
- user empathy
- measurable responsibility where it can be stated safely

The site should avoid:

- petition language
- private legal strategy
- confidential employer details
- internal metrics that are not approved for public use
- claims that cannot be backed up publicly

## Writing Pillars

### Product Systems

Explain how Basundara thinks about product surfaces as systems, not isolated screens. This pillar works well for Envoy-related writing because workplace operations require consistency across workflows.

### Trust And Reliability

Focus on how interfaces communicate state, change, and accountability. This creates a strong bridge between product design and operational outcomes.

### Practical Communication

Use short pieces that explain tradeoffs clearly. The writing should feel useful to product managers, designers, operators, and technical teams.

## Content Cadence

Start with three strong posts:

1. Envoy Field Notes: Designing for Operational Trust
2. Calm Interfaces for Busy Teams
3. The Practical Brief: Turning Research Into Action

Each post should be specific enough to show expertise, but general enough to avoid exposing private details.
